Current Laws

Currently there are no specific laws aiming to demystify the black box of AI with credit scores.

Some things that do exist are as follows:

  • Art. 22 GDPR – Automated individual decision-making, including profiling
  • Art. 7 GDPR – Conditions for consent
  • Art. 9 GDPR – Processing of special categories of personal data

Benefits

Synthetic Data Could be employed in synthetic data generation to counter bias and not raise issues against privacy.
Scenario Simulation Can simulate scenarios to help better prepare for institutional use in assessing risk of defaults.
Enhanced Credit Score Non-traditional data sources can be taken into account, such as transaction and social media history.
Fraud Detection Take it to a new level by detecting strange transactions or even anticipating with model patterns.

Statistics

AI in lending has grown from $9.18 billion in 2024 to $11.63 billion in 2025 at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 26.6%

Lawmakers

What needs to happen?

  • Laws that promote greater transparancy on AI involvement
  • Laws that help regulate how AI is used to avoid human involved data bias
  • Laws that allow individuals to take control of the data that is provided to AIs

Users

The best advice to users as of now would be to select companies carefully
